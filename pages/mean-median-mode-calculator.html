📚 Central Tendency

Mean, Median & Mode Explained

The three measures of central tendency describe the "center" of a dataset, but they do so differently:

  • Mean (μ) — Add all values, divide by count. Best for symmetric, unskewed data without outliers. μ = Σxᵢ / n
  • Median — Sort data, pick the middle value. Resistant to outliers. Best for skewed distributions (e.g., income data).
  • Mode — The value(s) that appear most often. Useful for categorical data or finding the most common outcome.
Mean = (x₁ + x₂ + ... + xₙ) / n

When to use which?

  • Symmetric data: Mean ≈ Median ≈ Mode
  • Right-skewed (outliers high): Mean > Median
  • Left-skewed (outliers low): Mean < Median
